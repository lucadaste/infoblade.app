How it works
01

We read the news

Every morning, InvestmentInformatics.AI ingests hundreds of global news sources — geopolitics, macro data, supply chains, earnings, natural events — and filters for what actually matters.

02

AI reasons through it

Our AI identifies which events carry real economic weight — then traces the full chain reaction from the event to supply and demand shifts, to sectors, to specific companies.

03

You get clarity

No jargon, no noise. A clear daily briefing: what happened, why it matters economically, and which sectors and stocks are in play — written for humans, not analysts.

Live example
Strait of Hormuz partially blocked by Iranian naval exercises
Reuters · May 6, 2026
Why it matters
Roughly 20% of global oil and LNG passes through the Strait of Hormuz daily. Any restriction creates immediate supply anxiety and has historically spiked Brent crude within 24–48 hours of similar events.
Sectors in play
Energy +++ Defense ++ Airlines --- Shipping -- Utilities ~
Likely beneficiaries
US domestic producers are insulated from Middle East supply disruption. Defense contractors often see elevated contract activity during periods of regional tension.

What you get

Daily event briefing

Every morning, the 5–10 most market-relevant global events — summarized with full economic reasoning, not just headlines. Know what moved while you slept.

Chain reaction analysis

We don't stop at the headline. We trace the full economic chain: event → supply/demand shift → sector impact → company-level exposure. Context is everything.

Sector + ticker signals

Each event maps to affected sectors and specific stocks or ETFs, with a clear signal: likely beneficiary, likely loser, or neutral — so you can act on what you learn.
